## Task 1 Write-up

For the first module, I was tasked with creating a Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic to educate my batchmates. I utilized Canva, a free browser-based design tool, to develop a one-page visual resource. My infographic covers three core topics: the definition of digital literacy, safe internet practices, and the importance of maintaining a professional online presence. The design uses clear visuals and concise text to ensure the message is easily digestible for first-year students. During the creation process, the most interesting aspect was learning how to distill complex cybersecurity and professional concepts into brief, engaging visual bullet points without losing their core meaning. However, I found it somewhat difficult to balance the visual elements with the text; avoiding a cluttered layout while still conveying all the necessary information required several iterations. Ultimately, this task enhanced my ability to communicate important digital concepts visually, a crucial skill for my role as a Digital Ambassador. *(167 words)*

## Task 4 Write-up

Effective online communication is a critical skill for students. Poor digital communication can cause significant real-world problems. For example, in a hypothetical scenario, a student might send an email to a professor asking for an extension but forget to include their name, registration number, or the specific assignment details. The professor, unable to identify the student or the context, ignores the email, resulting in the student failing the assignment. This situation highlights the necessity of structured communication. If the student had used a clear subject line, a professional greeting, provided all necessary context, and included a proper sign-off, the professor could have easily processed the request. This task reinforced that professional email etiquette—focusing on clarity, tone, and structure rather than length—is essential for avoiding misunderstandings and maintaining professional relationships. *(141 words)*

## Task 5 Write-up

While researching UPI and Online Payment Fraud for my cybercrime awareness case study, the most surprising discovery was how heavily these crimes rely on social engineering rather than complex technical hacking. It was alarming to learn how easily scammers manipulate victims into believing they need to enter a UPI PIN to receive money, exploiting simple human error and lack of awareness. Recognizing these online threats, one habit I will personally change as a result of this research is my approach to verifying online transactions. I will now rigorously scrutinize all payment requests, implement a strict "No PIN for receiving" personal rule, and ensure I always verify the identity of the person requesting funds. This awareness is vital for maintaining financial safety in our digitally-driven environment. *(137 words)*
