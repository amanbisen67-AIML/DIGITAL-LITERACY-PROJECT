
# 🛡️ Stay Safe Online: A Checklist for College Students

This checklist highlights 8 actionable steps to protect your digital identity and financial security while navigating academic and social life online.

### 📋 The Checklist

1.  **❌ No PIN for Receiving Money (UPI Safety):** Never enter your UPI PIN to *receive* a payment. If someone sends you a QR code or a "Request Money" notification claiming it will credit your account, it is a scam.
2.  **🔐 Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Activate 2FA on your university email, WhatsApp, and Instagram. This adds a critical layer of security even if your password is leaked.
3.  **🚫 Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking:** Do not log into bank accounts or sensitive student portals (like ERPs) using open Wi-Fi in cafes or hostels. Use your mobile data or a trusted VPN instead.
4.  **🕵️ Verify Internship/Job Offers:** Be wary of recruiters on WhatsApp or Telegram offering "Part-time jobs" or "Tasks" that require an initial "security deposit" or "registration fee." Legitimate companies never ask for money to hire you.
5.  **🎭 Use Masked Aadhaar:** When providing ID for hostel check-ins or event registrations, use a **Masked Aadhaar** (where only the last 4 digits are visible) to prevent identity theft.
6.  **📱 Audit App Permissions:** Regularly check your phone settings and revoke "Camera," "Microphone," and "Contacts" access for apps that don't strictly need them (e.g., a calculator or wallpaper app).
7.  **🛡️ Check Website Encryption:** Before entering your roll number or payment details, ensure the URL starts with **https://** and shows a padlock icon in the address bar.
8.  **🛑 Don't Post Sensitive Docs:** Avoid sharing photos of your Hall Ticket, Admit Card, or Internship Certificates on social media. These contain your DOB and registration numbers, which hackers use for "Social Engineering" attacks.

---

### 🚨 How to Report Cybercrime in India

If you or someone you know falls victim to a digital scam or harassment, use the following official channels immediately:

* **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [https://www.cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in)
* **National Helpline Number:** **1930** (Call this immediately for financial frauds to increase the chances of freezing the stolen funds).
