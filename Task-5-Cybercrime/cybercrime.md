# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## The "QR Code" Scam

### 1. The Crime
**UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud** is a digital financial crime where attackers manipulate the "Request Money" or "Scan and Pay" features of payment apps. Unlike traditional hacking, this relies on **Social Engineering**—the psychological manipulation of a user into authorizing a transaction they believe is a credit (receiving money), but is actually a debit (sending money).

### 2. Step-by-Step Execution
1.  **The Hook:** A fraudster poses as a buyer on a marketplace (like OLX or Facebook). They express urgent interest in an item the victim is selling to build a false sense of rapport.
2.  **The Bait:** To "pay" the victim, the attacker sends a QR code via WhatsApp, claiming it is for a "payment confirmation" or "advance deposit."
3.  **The Trap:** The attacker instructs the victim to scan the QR code and enter their **UPI PIN**. They use confusing language, such as "Scanning this is required to verify your account to receive 5,000 INR."
4.  **The Theft:** Once the victim enters their PIN, they have authorized a **debit request**. The money is instantly transferred from the victim's account to the attacker’s "mule" account.

### 3. Target Audience
* Individuals new to digital payment ecosystems.
* Senior citizens who may be less tech-savvy.
* Small-scale online sellers who are eager to close a sale.
* **Key Vulnerability:** Exploiting the common misconception that a PIN is needed to *receive* funds.

### 4. Consequences
* **Immediate Financial Loss:** Since the transaction is authorized with a valid PIN, banks often find it difficult to reverse the payment.
* **Emotional Distress:** Victims feel a sense of violation and embarrassment.
* **Loss of Trust:** Victims may become fearful of using digital banking systems, hindering their financial inclusion.
